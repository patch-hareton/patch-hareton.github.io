values + identity

28 -- they/them -- currently based in Te Whanganui a Tara

take me back home plz

I value equality- safety and dignity for all sentient life. I value autonomy/expression - the ability to live independently with creativity and freedom. I value connection/friendship/love - sharing meaningful experiences, being recognised by others and recognising them. I value personal (and collective) growth, the ability to explore the world and stay curious.

Working at Te Papa was a challenging experience, as my coworkers and especially those working at a lower level than me were treated extremely poorly by the managers. As someone who has been mistreated by a lot of employers before, and someone who reads a lot of Marxist theory, I’m passionate about workers’ rights, and also understood that as a supervisor I had more ability and responsibility to speak up for people who were less capable/confident in speaking up for themselves. As such I became the workplace Marxist and had frequent tensions with higher-ups in the company, but it was important to me to know I was using my skills and energy to help people who were being treated unfairly by their employer.

I can’t really think of what “my culture” would be, because I’ve lived on the edges of a lot of different ones. The culture I grew up in was extremely oppressive, and I guess I formed my alues and identity in rebellion of that. Since then I’ve found people and communities that are more in line with my values and have helped me strengthen and develop my sense of self and the world.

Love your hell by, love your hell by, love your hell by another name.
