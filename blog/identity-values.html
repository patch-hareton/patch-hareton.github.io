identity & values

I would identify some of my strengths as thoughtfulness, self-awareness, empathy and resilience. Some of my limitations are struggling with emotional balance, confusion, and maintaining passion for/commitment to things.

I think the last of these in particular is why I’ve struggled with learning things deeply and succeeding in a typical sense in any career. I have curiosity for a lot of things, but tell myself it isn’t worth engaging if I’m not totally passionate about them. As for career, I often get bored of what I’m doing and what I’m living, and start to daydream about other possibilities, rather than committing to the life I’ve already started building.

I think at 28 I’m pretty self-aware, and I can approach a challenge like DevAcademy with a bit more patience and grace, knowing the ways in which my mind will try to trip me up and having strategies to overcome those challenges.

I completed an Acting degree when I was a teenager, and a lot of the focus over the 3 years was artistic and academic collaboration. We had a lot of clashes as a class over the years, but we got better at communicating clearly and diplomatically and making compromises so that every person felt respected and supported. It helped having multiple people in the same situation, so that whenever there was a clash there were other people to rely on for support. We also understood that these conflicts were just part of collaborating on something we were all so passionate about, and that we all wanted the same thing for each other. Even though I did get a little better over the course, I could be quite fiery and undiplomatic in my approach to tension/conflict, and I think this is an area where I’ve grown a lot since.
